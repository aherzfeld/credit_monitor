High-Yield OAS
The spread (in basis points) between yields on US junk bonds and comparable Treasuries. This is the single most important real-time credit-stress signal — it reflects what bond investors are actually demanding to take on default risk. Spreads widen BEFORE defaults actually rise, so it's forward-looking. Rough guide: under 350bps is complacent, 400–500 is normal, 600+ signals stress, 1000+ is crisis.
